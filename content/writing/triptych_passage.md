It’s strange that tourists always want me to take their photos. My photo library is quite empty and more of my photos are in strangers’ libraries than my own 

I always had an aversion to taking photos on family vacations. Something about the process felt disingenuous to me. I thought that it spoiled the current moment. I felt that somehow, trying to capture the world for the future meant ruining it for me now. I despised the exhibitionism of the entire process. It made me feel watched. I could feel the eyes of the crowd streaming past us, piercing into my back. My Mom would motion for the rest of us to go up for a photo. We would stand awkwardly with smiles held too long plastered to our faces as she struggled with the camera. This eventually evolved into a selfie stick, except no selfies were taken. She would just put the phone on the selfie stick and hold it out. 

I never took much interest in the photo albums my mom produced every year. It was something that she stressed about and put a lot of time into every year. We would briefly flip through as a family and never look at it again. 

I went through one recently.

The people I stared at were strangers – a different family. I was barely recognizable. My sister was barely recognizable. The person staring through the photo felt distant. It was only me by name. There was only a tint of the familiar in the unknown. 

Those memories that felt so certain now feel estranged. My personal opinions and feelings have warped and transformed the memories I felt so certain about. It’s all just in my head. There’s nothing to ground me and my thoughts in reality. 

I know nothing about him. He kept no journal, took no photos. His interests are gone, hidden away in dusty shelves. His personal mementos are either gone or thrown away. His desk was always utilitarian. I want to know every intimate detail about him, but he has melted away.
